Reservoir Engineer (Ph.D., EIT)

I am a petroleum engineer with a research focus on reservoir engineering, data analytics, simulation and economic analysis of conventional and unconventional oil and gas formations. As a teaching assistant at Texas Tech University, I taught over 800 students during my MS and doctoral programs. I taught more than six classes, teaching some of them multiple times. During this period, I participated in several projects, some of which resulted in journal or conference publications (Google Scholar). Some of the projects were sponsored by oil and gas companies and professional organizations. My teaching interests include reservoir engineering, rock mechanics, rock and fluid properties, drilling engineering, pressure transient analysis, production engineering, oil and gas property evaluation and management, and oil and gas data analysis. My research interests include data analytics and web application development, machine learning, reservoir simulation, unconventional resources, reservoir fluid and rock properties, hydraulic fracturing, rock mechanics, and economic analysis/ petroleum economics. I am a reviewer for reputable journals like Elsevier’s Journal of Petroleum Science and Engineering, Taylor and Francis’ Cogent Engineering, and Springer’s Journal of Petroleum Exploration and Production Technology. I also reviewed conference papers and abstracts for American Rock Mechanics Association (ARMA) and Southwestern Petroleum Short Course (SWPSC). See List of Publications.
